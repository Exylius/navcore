# Exylius NavCore

Exylius R&D's NavCore is a cutting-edge research platform dedicated to the intricate task of modeling and navigating through space. It achieves this by employing a singular, physics-driven coordinate system that unifies celestial bodies. The platform seamlessly blends actual astronomical data with sophisticated simulation tools, while also delving into autonomous navigation techniques, notably those utilizing pulsars for positioning.

## What NavCore Does

At its heart, NavCore offers a robust framework for depicting the positions and movements of stars within a galactic Cartesian coordinate system. By merging real-world astronomical observations with simulation capabilities, it opens avenues for exploring space navigation, understanding spatial relationships, and developing autonomous localization methods for extraterrestrial environments.

## Key Capabilities

- **A Universal Stellar Coordinate System (USCS):** A standardized way to represent star positions.  
- **Coordinate Transformation:** Converts traditional Right Ascension, Declination, and Distance into Cartesian coordinates.  
- **Real Star Data Integration:** Incorporates actual stellar data, such as that from the Gaia mission.  
- **Pulsar Localization Simulation:** Models and tests navigation using pulsar signals.  
- **Advanced Navigation Models:** Features experimental approaches to navigation and pathfinding.  

## Research Objectives

NavCore is specifically engineered to tackle fundamental challenges in space navigation:

1. Establishing a universal, physics-based coordinate system for space.  
2. Enabling navigation within a constantly changing galactic environment populated by moving celestial objects.  
3. Developing methods for determining location without reliance on external infrastructure.  

## Project Organization

The project is structured into several key components:

- `/core`: Houses the core logic for coordinate systems, mathematical operations, and navigation algorithms.  
- `/simulation`: Contains modules for simulating pulsar signals, noise, and conducting experiments.  
- `/data`: Stores astronomical datasets and star catalogs.  
- `/docs`: Holds research notes and project documentation.  

## Current Development Stage

This is an early-stage research and prototyping effort. The systems are actively being developed and may undergo significant changes.

## Future Outlook

The ultimate vision for NavCore is to become a cornerstone system for research into interstellar navigation, autonomous positioning, and the creation of large-scale spatial models. Its potential applications extend to future space exploration missions and advanced simulation environments.
